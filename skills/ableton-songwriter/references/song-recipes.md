# Song Recipes By Genre Family

Use these defaults when details are missing.

## Genre Defaults

1. Electronic / Dance
- BPM: 120-132
- Rhythm: four-on-floor or syncopated groove
- Sections: intro, build, drop/main

2. Pop
- BPM: 95-125
- Rhythm: straight or light syncopation
- Sections: verse, pre, chorus, post

3. Hip-hop / Trap
- BPM: 70-85 or 130-170 double-time feel
- Rhythm: programmed kick patterns, snare/clap pocket
- Sections: intro, verse, hook

4. RnB / Soul
- BPM: 70-110
- Rhythm: laid-back groove, softer transients
- Sections: verse, hook, bridge

5. Rock / Indie
- BPM: 95-150
- Rhythm: live-band pocket
- Sections: verse, chorus, bridge

6. Ambient / Cinematic
- BPM: 60-100
- Rhythm: sparse pulse or no explicit groove
- Sections: evolving layers and motifs

7. Singer-songwriter
- BPM: 70-120
- Rhythm: simple supportive groove
- Sections: verse, chorus, optional bridge

## Section Recipes

1. 16-bar sketch:
- 4 bars intro
- 4 bars section A
- 8 bars section B

2. 32-bar full idea:
- 4 bars intro
- 8 bars section A
- 4 bars pre or turnaround
- 8 bars section B
- 8 bars extension or alternate B

3. 64-bar extended idea:
- Repeat 32-bar flow with variation in drums, bass, and top line.

## Contrast Rules

- Section A: fewer layers, lower high-end intensity.
- Section B: fuller rhythm, stronger hook, denser arrangement.
- Maintain one clear motif so sections feel connected.

## Quick Mix Heuristics

- Set drums and bass first.
- Keep low end centered.
- Pan support layers lightly for width.
- Use gentle compression and saturation where style calls for it.
- Preserve headroom for later mastering.

## Safe Defaults

- Genre: Pop/Electronic hybrid
- BPM: 120
- Key: A minor
- Length: 32 bars
- Mix target: balanced modern loud
